Penny Hardaway’s Early Life

Born in Memphis, Tennessee, Hardaway grew up with a love for basketball. He played high school basketball at Treadwell High School in Memphis and later attended the University of Memphis, where he continued to excel in the sport.

Professional Basketball Career

Hardaway’s professional career began when he was selected by the Golden State Warriors in the 1993 NBA Draft. However, he was immediately traded to the Orlando Magic, where he played alongside Shaquille O’Neal. His time with the Magic was marked by numerous accolades, including four NBA All-Star selections.

Phoenix Suns and New York Knicks

In 1999, Hardaway was traded to the Phoenix Suns, where he continued to perform at a high level. However, injuries began to take a toll on his career. He was later traded to the New York Knicks in 2004, but his performance was hampered by persistent injuries.

Miami Heat and Retirement

Hardaway’s final stint in the NBA was with the Miami Heat. He retired from professional basketball in 2007, ending a career that spanned over a decade.

Coaching Career

After retiring from professional basketball, Hardaway turned to coaching. He started his coaching career at the high school level before moving on to college basketball. In 2018, he was named the head coach of the Memphis Tigers men’s basketball team, a position he still holds.

Endorsements and Business Ventures

Like many professional athletes, Hardaway has made a significant amount of his wealth through endorsements and business ventures. Real Estate Investments

Philanthropy
