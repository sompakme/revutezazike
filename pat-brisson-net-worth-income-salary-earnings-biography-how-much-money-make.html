Pat Brisson (born January 22, 1965 in Salaberry-de-Valleyfield, Quebec, Canada) is an NHLPA agent and co-head of the Hockey Division of Creative Artists Agency, along with partner J. P. Barry. Brisson is a member of the Canadian Hockey League Players Association (NHLPA). Brisson was ranked the 12th most influential agent in all of sports by the Sports Business Journal in 2008, and he was also named one of the top 100 most powerful individuals in the National Hockey League (NHL) by The Hockey News in the same year. With a client list that includes three straight first-round selections in the NHL draught, Brisson has a strong reputation in the league (Sidney Crosby in 2005, Erik Johnson in 2006, Patrick Kane in 2007). In the summer of 2007, Brisson negotiated contracts worth more than $150 million on behalf of his clients, including a $52 million, eight-year agreement with Daniel Brière.   He has collaborated with players such as Jaromir Jagr, Sergei Federov, and Mats Sundin, who are among the highest-paid players in NHL history. Brisson also represents Nathan MacKinnon, who was selected first overall in the 2013 NHL Draft. Brisson established “Skate with the Pros” and “California Dreamin” in the early 1990s, respectively, which are kids hockey academies that include NHL players and coaches serving as teachers. Because of his participation with Iceoplex, a network of ice rinks in Southern California, he was a pivotal role in the development of the sport in the region. As a junior hockey player in the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League (QMJHL), Brisson averaged more than a point per game with the Verdun Juniors, Drummondville Voltigeurs, and Hull Olympiques, where he played for future NHL coach Pat Burns and skated alongside NHL legend Luc Robitaille.
