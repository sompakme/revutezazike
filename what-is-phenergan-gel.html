What is Phenergan gel?

Answer

Promethazine (commonly known as Phenergan®) is a kind of medication known as an antiemetic. It is used in the treatment of nausea and vomiting. Promethazine topical gel is a convenient method to provide this nausea medication to your kid.

 

Then there’s the subject of how to apply Phenergan gel.

Promethazine transdermal gel may be applied to the wrist every 4-6 hours as required to relieve nausea and vomiting caused by motion sickness or pregnancy (or as directed by the prescriber). Washing your hands before and after applying medicine is very essential. This is particularly true when you are delivering medication to another person, such as a little kid.

 

As a result, the question is: how much phenergan gel am I allowed to consume?

It is recommended that children and adults take 25 mg of Phenergan on an average daily basis for the active treatment of nausea and vomiting. When oral medicine cannot be tolerated, the dosage should be administered parenterally (see also ).

How long does it take for the effects of Phenergan to take effect?

Promethazine will begin to make you feel drowsy around 20 minutes after you take it and may continue to work for up to 12 hours after that.
