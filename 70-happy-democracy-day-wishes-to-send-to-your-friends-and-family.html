What is the meaning of Democracy Day?

Every year, Nigeria celebrates Democracy Day, a public holiday that commemorates the country's return to democracy. The event that is currently observed every year occurred in 1999 when Olusegun Obasanjo was democratically elected the President of Nigeria, ending the country's decades-long military rule.

When is Democracy Day in Nigeria?

Democracy Day is celebrated on June 12th every year.

Why was Democracy Day changed to June 12?

Democracy Day in Nigeria was changed to June 12th in 2018 to commemorate a significant event in the country's democratic journey. Previously, Democracy Day was celebrated on May 29th, marking the return to civilian rule in 1999. The new date, June 12th, honors the widely believed free and fair presidential election that took place on that day in 1993.

Why do you celebrate Democracy Day in Nigeria?

Nigerians do celebrate Democracy Day to commemorate the restoration of democracy in 1999.
