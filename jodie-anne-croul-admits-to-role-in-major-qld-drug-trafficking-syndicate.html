Jodie Anne Croul admits to role in major Qld drug trafficking syndicate

A Brisbane woman who headed up a drug trafficking syndicate that peddled heroin and illegal pharmaceutical drugs faces a lengthy wait for her sentencing while she enters rehab.

Jodie Anne Croul pleaded guilty to multiple charges, including drug trafficking, during her brief arraignment before Brisbane Supreme Court on Wednesday.

It comes more than three years after police dismantled the syndicate during a lengthy operation back in 2019.

Croul, dressed in a pink cardigan, pleaded guilty to nine charges, including trafficking dangerous drugs and possessing the proceeds of drug trafficking.

She trafficked drugs between November 2018 and May 2019 in Boondall, in Brisbane’s north.

The charges were laid after police raided properties across the River City back in 2019, charging 24 people with drug-related offences.

Dramatic footage released by Queensland Police showed officers smashing their way into one property allegedly linked to the syndicate.

“During Operation Quebec Viper detectives successfully used a number of covert strategies alongside traditional investigative techniques to progress the investigation,” Queensland Police said in a statement released in May 2019.

“Police will allege the drug trafficking syndicate, which dealt in various drugs such as heroin and illegal pharmaceutical drugs, has been successfully dismantled.”

Supreme Court Justice Martin Burns did not set a fixed date to sentence Croul after submissions from her barrister.

The court was told she would be entering a rehab centre next week and had a further appointment with her psychiatrist in April.

“She has paid the deposit … we could list it for sentence some time down the track in June,” defence barrister Christopher Wilson said.

Justice Martin instead listed Croul’s case for review on March 29.

Her bail was continued.
