Early Life and Career Beginnings

Sheek Louch was born in Brooklyn, New York, and grew up in Yonkers. He developed a passion for music at a young age and started his career as a member of The LOX in 1994. The group was initially signed to Bad Boy Records, where they released their debut album, “Money, Power & Respect.”

Success with The LOX

The LOX gained significant recognition in the late 90s, contributing to the success of Bad Boy Records. Their debut album peaked at number three on the Billboard 200 and was certified platinum. The group later left Bad Boy Records and signed with Ruff Ryders, where they released their second album, “We Are the Streets.”

Solo Career

Sheek Louch launched his solo career in 2003 with the release of his debut album, “Walk Witt Me,” under Universal Records.
