The world's most exotic rivers

The Napo, Ecuador

A journey along the Napo River, through the 6,500 square miles of Ecuador's Yasuni National Park, brings the planet's great green lung into vivid focus. Here are creepers, vines, soaring canopies, orchids, palms and ferns, as well as turtles, dolphins, monkeys, macaws, caimans and snakes. The lily-livered have never had easier access to this crucible of life at its most intense as the luxurious Anakonda Amazon has been breezing along the river in considerable style for a few years now. Book through Sunvil Latin America (020 8758 4774; sunvil.co.uk).

The Telegraph is working in partnership with Steppes Travel on an exclusive itinerary travelling deep in the remote Ecuadorian region of the Amazon with explorer and film-maker Benedict Allen. The trip takes readers off the beaten track to swim with pink dolphins, meet isolated indigenous communities and encounter the array of wildlife in Cuyabeno Wildlife Reserve, all the while staying in five-star luxury on board the 18-suite Anakonda river ship.
