How old is Paige VanZant and what's her net worth?

PAIGE VanZant is one of the most popular female combat sports stars in the world.

In November 2022, the model shared some racy photos in leather lingerie.

Who is mixed martial arts fighter Paige VanZant?

VanZant was born Paige Sletten on March 26, 1994, in Dundee, Oregon.

Growing up, her parents owned a dance studio, where she trained in ballet, jazz and hip hop for thirteen years.

She also starred in a Bissell mop commercial as a teen.

She was considered a tomboy growing up and loved riding dirt bikes, fishing, and being outdoors.

Paige legally changed her last name from Sletten to VanZant due to persistent bullies in high school.

She has noted that her experiences with bullying as one of the reasons she enjoyed learning mixed martial arts, and being able to defend herself.

After high school, her family moved to Nevada, where she tried to find a dance studio but instead came across UFC veteran Ken Shamrock's gym.

She began to train in MMA and boxing, leading her to win an amateur fight at 18.

VanZant made her professional MMA debut in 2012.

She signed a deal to fight under the All Elite Wrestling (AEW) banner in 2022.

In 2018 she married fellow MMA fighter Austin Vanderford.

When was Paige VanZant in the UFC?

In 2013, VanZant and ten other women signed with Ultimate Fighting Championship's (UFC) newly created Strawweight Division.

She made her UFC debut in 2014 in a knockout victory against Kailin Curran.

After defeating Curran, VanZant saw a rise in popularity and landed a sponsorship deal with Reebok.

The deal proved controversial because the 5-foot-4 beauty had only one fight up until that point.

Other fighters and the media speculated she was being favored due to her looks.

UFC president Dana White defended the Reebok deal, saying it came because of VanZant's "it" factor and personality.

In 2020, VanZant announced she was leaving UFC to pursue a more lucrative fighting career.

She told Heavy that she wants to retire from combat sports someday, so she wants to make as much money as possible while she still can.

Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship (BKFC) signed VanZant to a four-fight exclusive contract.

She made her debut for BKFC in a losing effort against former boxer Britain Hart in February 2021.

She hasn't hasn't fought in BKFC since losing to Rachel Ostovich in July 2021.

What were some of Paige VanZant's television appearances?

In 2016, VanZant was announced for the 22nd season of popular dance competition show Dancing With The Stars.

She was paired with professional dancer Mark Ballas and placed second.

VanZant was outspoken about making more money on DWTS than her entire time with UFC.

A year later the fighter appeared on the Food Network celebrity cooking competition show Chopped.

She was featured on the episode "Star Powers: Culinary Muscle" with NFL star LaMarr Woodley, Olympic gold medallist fencer Mariel Zagunis, and Olympic gold medalist figure skater Dorothy Hamill.

How many Instagram followers does Paige VanZant have?

VanZant made headlines in 2019 when she said she makes more money "sitting at home posting pictures on Instagram than I do fighting".

As of 2022 the former dancer has 3.2 million Instagram followers.

She also said: “I’ve added up all the money I’ve made in the UFC over six years, and I could have just had a regular job with the same pay."

What is Paige VanZant's net worth?

According to FightFans, VanZant is approximately worth $1.5million as of 2022.

The 26-year-old bombshell launched her exclusive subscription based website PaigeFanZant.com.

Fans pay $9.99 monthly for photos, videos, and access to the star.

According to ESPN's Ariel Helwani, VanZant's BKFC deal is worth over $1million plus incentives, a "significant raise" from what she was making in UFC.

VanZant also released an autobiography in 2018 titled Rise: Surviving the Fight of My Life.

She has modeled for Nike, Columbia Footwear, Reebok and has appeared in the 2019 Sports Illustrated swimsuit issue.

VanZant's endorsements include Monster Energy Drinks, Metro PCS, and Harley Davidson.
