Laura Nyro /nro/ NEAR-oh was an American composer, singer, and pianist who lived from October 18, 1947 until April 8, 199Her own records, especially Eli and the Thirteenth Confession (1968) and New York Tendaberry (1969), received critical praise, and she enjoyed commercial success with musicians such as Barbra Streisand and The 5th Dimension covering her songs. Her sound was a mix of New York pop, jazz, gospel, rhythm and blues, show songs, rock, and soul, as well as Brill Building-style New York pop. The 5th Dimension had hits with “Blowing Away,” “Wedding Bell Blues,” “Stoned Soul Picnic,” “Sweet Blindness,” “Save the Country,” and “Black Patch” between 1968 and 1970; Blood, Sweat & Tears and Peter, Paul & Mary with “And When I Die”; Three Dog Night and Maynard Ferguson with “Eli’s Comin'”; and Barbra Streisand with “Stoney End,” “Time and Love Nyro’s version of Carole King and Gerry Goffin’s “Up on the Roof” was her best-selling single. Nyro was elected to the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in 2012.
