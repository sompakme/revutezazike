IT was Martha Gellhorn who persuaded Ernest Hemingway to come with her to observe the Spanish Civil War. It was she who saw the carnage of the D-Day beaches while he stayed safely on a ship. As a war reporter, she outshone him; as a novelist, he put her in the shade - and the rivalry between them outlived their nine-year relationship, which ended in 1946.
