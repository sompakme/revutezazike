Canelo Alvarez makes big screen debut in 'Creed III' trailer, directed by Michael B. Jordan

The new 'Creed III' trailer is finally here and Canelo Alvarez makes a small appearance in it, a film that marks Michael B. Jordan's diretorial debut. As part of the Rocky franchise, Creed has managed to create a life of its own with a complete trilogy happening already. Although 'Creed II' wasn't as critically acclaimed as the first one, this new story seems far more personal for the titular character as he faces a friend from his childhood that will be played by Jonathan Majors.

In the trailer, Canelo appears with Adonis Creed's family during a red carpet event. Back in the middle of September, it was announced that the Mexican boxer was cast to play himself in the movie with an unspecified role. But making it to the trailer could mean he does have a more prominent role in the movie. If you blink, you'll miss Canelo in the trailer.

Jonathan Majors will play Damian

The actor that had his big break in Spike Lee's 'Da 5 Bloods' from 2020 is getting another role as a villain with Michael B. Jordan as his opposite in the film. From what we could see in the trailer, Majors plays Damian. Who seems to be Creed's childhood friend that got into some sort of trouble and ultimately imprisoned.

Given that Creed didn't seem to do time in jail, it appears Majors took the fall and that got him to do almost two decades behind bars. This already seems like a back-to-basics kind of story for the Creed franchise that sadly won't have Sylvester Stallone reprising his role as Rocky Balboa. Just last year, Stallone himself explained why he wasn't returning as his legendary character for this movie.

During a small exchange with Metro UK, Sly revealed that the story Michael B. Jordan was going to tell was going in a different direction. It doesn't follow the story of Rocky and Stallone had to step down from the project. This is what the legendary actor said: "Well it's different because, actually, I bowed out of that one, I just didn't know if there was any part for me. It takes it in a different direction. I wish them well and keep punching!" Despite not acting in the film, Stallone remains a producer of the new Creed installment.
