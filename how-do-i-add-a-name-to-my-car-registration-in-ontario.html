How Do I Add A Name To My Car Registration In Ontario

You and Your Beloved...Car? Adding a Name to Your Ontario Car Registration (It's Not Marriage, But It's a Commitment)

Let's face it, in Ontario, owning a car is practically a right of passage. But what happens when you want to share the joy (and maybe some of the gas money) with your significant other, bestie, or that overly enthusiastic sibling who keeps borrowing your wheels? That's where adding a name to your car registration comes in.

Before You Hit the Road (to ServiceOntario)

Adding a name isn't quite like giving your car a cool nickname (although, feel free to brainstorm some awesome ones while you're at it). There are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Are you sure you want to do this? Sharing car ownership is a big step. This person will now be legally entitled to drive your precious automobile. Use this time to ponder deep philosophical questions like: "Do they parallel park like a champ?" or "Will they secretly eat all the french fries on road trips?"
  • Gather your paperwork. This isn't rocket science, but you'll need your original vehicle permit, a piece of valid ID (driver's license, anyone?), and proof of valid Ontario car insurance (because safety first, folks). Bonus points if you can convince your co-owner to bring their ID too, just to save everyone some time.

At ServiceOntario: The Name Game

Alright, you've made it to the promised land (otherwise known as your local ServiceOntario). Now comes the fun part:

  • Head to the counter and explain your mission. Don't worry, they won't ask you to reenact a car chase scene from an action movie. Just politely tell them you want to add a name to your vehicle registration.

  • The lovely folks at ServiceOntario will take your documents and perform some bureaucratic magic. This might involve a bit of waiting, so feel free to use this time to:

    • Mentally rehearse that cool car nickname.
    • Bond with your co-owner over your shared love of the open road (or hatred of traffic jams).
    • Plan your next epic road trip adventure.

  • Pay the fee (it's a cool $32, a small price for co-ownership bliss).

  • Voila! You're now the proud co-owner of a car with (hopefully) a responsible co-pilot.

Important Note: If your name has recently changed due to marriage, divorce, or just a deep desire to reinvent yourself, you'll need to bring some additional documents proving your name change. But hey, at least you get a cool new story to tell about your car!

FAQ: Adding a Name to Your Ontario Car Registration

How to find my nearest ServiceOntario location?

The wonders of the internet! You can search for your closest ServiceOntario on the Ontario government website: https://www.ontario.ca/locations/serviceontario

How much does it cost to add a name to my car registration?

It'll set you back a cool $32.

Do I need a safety certificate to add a name?

Nope, no safety certificate required.

What if my co-owner doesn't have a driver's license?

They don't necessarily need a driver's license to be added to the registration, but they will need some form of valid ID.

How long does it take to add a name to the registration?

Typically, it can be done in one visit to ServiceOntario, although wait times can vary.

So there you have it! Adding a name to your car registration is a breeze. Now get out there and conquer the open road (responsibly, of course) with your newfound co-owner!
