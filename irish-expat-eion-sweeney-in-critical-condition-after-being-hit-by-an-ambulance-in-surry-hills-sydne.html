Irish expat Eion Sweeney in critical condition after being hit by an ambulance in Surry Hills, Sydne

An Irish expat is fighting for his life after he was hit by an ambulance in Sydney’s east over the weekend.

Emergency services were called to Elizabeth St in Surry Hills when Eion Sweeney, 32, was struck by the vehicle shortly after midnight on Sunday.

Paramedics treated Mr Sweeney at the scene for serious head injuries and he was rushed to Royal Prince Alfred Hospital in a critical condition.

It is believed the ambulance crew was responding to an urgent call for assistance.

A candlelight vigil was held in Mr Sweeney’s north Ireland hometown of Clonmany on Sunday night, where prayers were held for him and 10 other people who died in a separate incident in a petrol station explosion.

“Thank you to everyone who joined our candlelight vigil this evening,” the Clonmany Together Facebook group said.

“Can we please ask for prayers for Eoin Sweeney from Ballyliffin, who was in an accident in Australia, thank you.”

A GoFundMe page has also been set up by one of Mr Sweeney’s friend, which has raised nearly $90,000.

“Our very well-known and loved friend Eoin Sweeney from Clonmany, Co. Donegal was in an accident on Saturday night and his family are currently on their way here to Sydney, Australia to be by his side for recovery,” they said.

“He is currently in an induced coma in Royal Prince Alfred Hospital and we are hoping to raise funds to help with all medical expenses and aftercare.”

Mr Sweeney was in a critical but stable condition as of Monday.

NSW Police established a crime scene following the crash and have commenced inquiries into the circumstances surrounding the incident.
