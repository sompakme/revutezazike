People In Medieval Times Wore Shockingly Long Shoes That Even Elves Would Laugh About

published on August 20, 2024
Today, there are different styles of pointy shoes for men and women. The pointiness of modern shoes, however, is no match for the crakows that gained popularity in Europe in the 14th century. The name crakow came from the city of Kraków, Poland, where the shoe style originally came from. Crakows were also called by some as poulaines, and the elongated portion was usually filled with stuffing materials, such as moss or wool, to retain its shape, as noted by the MET Museum.
